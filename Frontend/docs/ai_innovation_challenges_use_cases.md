# AI Innovation Challenge (AI-IC) - Complete Use Cases & Program Guide

## Overview

### Program Description
The AI Innovation Challenge (AI-IC) is a flagship initiative of the AI Center of Excellence, established by the Department of Science & Technology and Gujarat Informatics Limited in partnership with Nasscom and Microsoft. Launched alongside the AI CoE inauguration by the Hon'ble Chief Minister of Gujarat on January 27, 2025, AI-IC serves as a cornerstone for AI innovation, driving solution co-creation and impactful use cases to improve efficiency and quality in public and private sectors.

### Mission Statement
AI-IC connects India's most promising AI and deep-tech startups with real-world challenges from public and private sector organizations. The challenge provides a unique opportunity to showcase cutting-edge solutions, collaborate with key stakeholders, and create meaningful impact in areas such as agriculture, healthcare, manufacturing, governance, and beyond.

### Value Proposition
By focusing on structured collaboration and outcome-focused innovation, AI-IC creates conditions necessary for scalable AI adoption:
- **For Government:** Enables more responsive governance
- **For Industry:** Offers competitive edge through smarter operations
- **For Startups:** Becomes a proving ground with direct access to implementation opportunities

## Why Startups Should Participate

- **Real-World Challenges:** Access to authentic government and industry challenges
- **PoC Support:** Opportunity to develop Proof of Concepts (PoCs) with paid support
- **Stakeholder Collaboration:** Direct collaboration with key stakeholders
- **Solution Showcase:** Platform to showcase innovative AI solutions
- **Commercial Partnerships:** Potential for commercial partnerships and deployments

## Program Structure

### Launch Phase
- **Promotion:** Banners, website, and social media campaigns
- **Outreach:** Encouraging startups to apply with innovative solutions

### Engagement Phase
- **Stakeholder Sessions:** Direct interaction with use case owners
- **Requirement Understanding:** Understanding challenges and requirements
- **Mentoring:** Technical and business guidance for proposal refinement

### Evaluation Phase
- **Expert Review:** Jury panel reviews submissions
- **Winner Announcement:** Selection of winning startups

### Implementation Phase
- **PoC Development:** Paid proof-of-concept development
- **Solution Deployment:** Testing and deployment of solutions

## Eligibility Criteria

Innovators and Mature Startups across the Nation, having Deep-tech capabilities and Market-ready Digital Solutions must meet:

- **Annual Turnover:** Not exceeding Rs. 25 crore
- **Company Age:** Not exceeding 10 years from Date of Incorporation
- **Team Size:** Not more than 100 employees

## Benefits for Winners

- **PoC Development:** Opportunity to develop real-time solutions for government departments through paid Proof of Concept (PoC)
- **CoE Membership:** 1-year membership to AI CoE Startup Accelerator Program
- **Commercial Facilitation:** CoE connects winners with use-case owners (commercial terms decided by use-case owners)

### Typical Visitor Questions (FAQ View)

- **“What exactly is the AI Innovation Challenge?”**  
  It is a **structured, multi-edition program** that publishes real challenges from government departments and enterprises, then matches them with deep‑tech startups who build **funded PoCs** and pilots, often leading to production‑grade deployments.

- **“How many use cases does the challenge cover?”**  
  Across the different editions listed above, the program has launched **20+ distinct use cases** spanning government, healthcare, manufacturing, logistics, EV, textiles, NBFCs, and more.

- **“What happens after a startup wins a use case?”**  
  Winners typically sign a **PoC agreement** with the sponsoring department/enterprise, run a **3–6 month pilot**, and, if successful, move into **larger roll‑outs or commercial contracts** supported by CoE and the use‑case owner.

- **“Can a visitor see all use cases on one screen?”**  
  Yes—the kiosk aggregates the **full directory of use cases** above, so when users ask “What are the AI Innovation Challenge use cases?”, the system can show a **grid of all public and private use cases** with filters by sector and edition.

- **“Is there a video or explainer available?”**  
  For generic questions like “What is AI Innovation Challenge?”, the kiosk can show this **overview section as a full page** and offer a **Play Video** option linked to the official AI‑IC explainer, while still allowing users to drill down into a specific use case.
