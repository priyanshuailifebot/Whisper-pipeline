# Gujarat IT/ITeS Policy (2022-27) - Complete Incentives & Regulatory Framework

## Policy Overview

### Document Reference
**Government of Gujarat**  
**Department of Science and Technology**  
**G.R. No: ITP/10/2021/583612/IT**  
**Sachivalaya, Gandhinagar**  
**Date: 11/10/2024**

### Policy Scope
This policy provides a comprehensive framework for promoting Information Technology and Information Technology Enabled Services (IT/ITeS) sector in Gujarat, with special emphasis on Deep Technologies including Artificial Intelligence, IoT, Blockchain, and other emerging technologies.

### Operative Period
The policy is effective from February 7, 2022, and remains operative until March 31, 2027.

## Section 2: Definitions

### 2.5.3 IT Enabled Services (ITeS) - Revised Definition

**IT enabled Services (ITeS)** are construed as any service, which results from the use of any IT software over a system of IT products for realising value addition service rendering through the application of IT and shall include the following:

#### A. Emerging or Deep Technologies
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Including Natural Language Processing (NLP), Machine Learning (ML), Generative AI
- **Internet of Things (IoT)**
- **Analytics using AI technologies:** Including Big Data & Data Science
- **Augmented Reality (AR), Virtual Reality (VR) & Mixed Reality (MR)**
- **Distributed Ledger Technology (Blockchain)**
- **Quantum Computing**

#### B. Back-office operation/Business Process Outsourcing (BPO)

#### C. Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KPO)

#### D. Web/digital content development ERP/software and application development

#### E. Bioinformatics

#### F. Banking, Financial Services and Insurance (BFSI) services

#### G. Depository, security registration and dematerialisation services

#### H. GIS-enabled services

#### I. IT support centers

#### J. Website services

#### K. Global In-house Center (GIC) / Global Capability Center (GCC)

Any software or application that provides any service or utility through the use of AI, where AI algorithms are employed where GPU/TPU is necessary to run the algorithm, will be considered. However, offering generic application services, such as chatbots, will not be considered under section 2.5.3 (A) of the policy.

### Key Definitions Added/Revised

#### 2.5.9 Gross Fixed Capital Investment (GFCI)
Expenditure made in the construction of the building, **purchase of the building**, computers, software, networking related hardware and other related fixed assets, **excluding the cost of land** required to produce products or services by the eligible unit.

#### 2.5.11 Eligible Unit
Any new unit having a minimum of **50 employees** on its payroll or an expansion unit having a minimum of **75 employees** on its payroll. **A workstation, laptop or any IT device should be allocated to a minimum 80% of its total employees.**

#### 2.5.16 Data Center
A facility that centralizes computing and networking equipment to collect, store, process and disseminate a large amount of data and applications. Incentives will only be provided for setting up tier three or above Data Centers in the state subject to:
- A minimum built-up area of 4,000 sq. ft.
- A minimum of 150 racks or IT power consumption of at least 900 KW in racks or 2,00,000 processor cores.

#### 2.5.26 E. Patent Expenditure
Expenditure incurred on the application of every successful patent during the operative period of the Policy up to **INR 10 lakhs per patent**.

#### 2.5.27 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Incubator
An organization established under the Companies Act/LLP Act, engaged in supporting ICT & deep tech start-ups through host of support services and resources such as deep tech labs, POC facilities, space, funding support, mentoring, and business management services. Minimum 10 Startups incubated at a time, registered under this policy.

#### 2.5.28 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Accelerator
An organization set up to empower ICT & deep tech startups by providing advanced resources and technological support, enhancing their market-ready solutions, market linkages while fortifying their business strategies. Must have 3+ years experience and supported 20+ startups with at least 5 having received non-public investment.

#### 2.5.29 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Startup
Must meet Government of India startup definition, turnover not exceeding INR 100 Cr, not formed by reconstruction, working towards innovation/development/improvement of products/processes/services with scalable business model. Eligible till employee count exceeds 10 or turnover exceeds INR 100 Cr.

#### 2.5.30 Global In-house Center (GIC) / Global Capability Center (GCC)
Offshore centres established by companies for providing various services to parent organizations. Minimum GFCI of INR 25 Cr. required, with 75% operations through deep tech and ICT technologies.

#### 2.5.31 GPU
A stand-alone graphics processing unit for handling large set of data installed separately from the Central Processing Unit (CPU). GPU embedded in CPU shall not be considered.

#### 2.5.32 Technical Committee
Committee constituted by DST headed by Director ICT & e-Governance for validation and scrutiny of eligible entities working in deep tech subsector.

## Section 2.7: Incentives and Offerings

### 2.7.1 Fiscal Incentive Support

#### Category I - GFCI less than INR 250 Cr.

**CAPEX Support:**
- Up to **20%** of expenditure made in construction and purchase of building, other related fixed assets
- Up to **30%** of computers, software, networking related hardware
- Maximum ceiling of INR 50 Cr.
- Disbursement in 20 equal quarterly instalments

**OPEX Support:**
- Up to 15% of annual eligible OPEX expenditure
- Maximum ceiling of INR 20 Cr. per year for 5 years
- Total expenditure on Bandwidth and Cloud Rental capped at 35% of eligible OPEX or INR 7 crore per annum, whichever lower
- Disbursement in quarterly instalments

#### Category II (Mega Projects) - GFCI ≥ INR 250 Cr. or ≥ 500 direct IT employment

**CAPEX Support:**
- Up to 20% of expenditure made in construction and purchase of building, other related fixed assets
- Up to 30% of computers, software, networking related hardware
- Maximum ceiling of INR 200 Cr.
- Disbursement in 20 equal quarterly instalments

**OPEX Support:**
- Up to 15% of annual eligible OPEX expenditure
- Maximum ceiling of INR 40 Cr. per year for 5 years
- Bandwidth and Cloud Rental capped at 35% of eligible OPEX or INR 14 crore per annum, whichever lower
- Disbursement in quarterly instalments

**Additional Provisions:**
- Building construction expenditure capped at INR 3,000/sq. ft. for 60 sq. ft. per employee
- Annual CAPEX assistance capped to total annual operating expenditure
- Total CAPEX + OPEX + Special Incentives shall not exceed 75% of eligible GFCI

### 2.7.2 Special Incentives for IT/ITeS Units

Available to:
- 2.5.11 Eligible units
- 2.5.16 Data centers
- 2.5.27 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Incubators
- 2.5.28 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Accelerators
- 2.5.29 Eligible ICT & Deep Tech Startups
- 2.5.30 Global In-house Center (GIC) / Global Capability Center (GCC)
- 2.7.4 A. Deep Tech Centre of Excellence

Excluding IT/ITeS Park, IT City/Township, and 2.5.17 R&D Centre.

### 2.7.11 Incentives for Deep Tech

- **One-third relaxation** in eligibility criteria for entities working in deep tech subsectors (section 2.5.3 A)
- **Extended depreciation period** (5 years) for expenditure made in deep tech IT hardware
- **Annexure A** provides indicative list of hardware and software requirements

### 2.7.12 Incentives for ICT & Deep Tech Incubators, Accelerators, and Startups

**Note:** Entities taking benefits under Startup Policies shall not be eligible for benefits as an Eligible ICT & deep tech startup under this policy.

#### For ICT & Deep Tech Incubators
**CAPEX Support:** Up to 25% of eligible CAPEX, maximum INR 25 Cr., disbursed in 20 quarterly instalments

**OPEX Support:** Up to 15% of Lease Rental, 15% Power tariff, 20% Bandwidth, 20% Cloud rental expenditure, maximum INR 10 Cr. per year for 5 years

#### For ICT & Deep Tech Accelerators
10% assistance on investments facilitated through venture capitalists to ICT & deep tech startups, capped at INR 10 lakhs per startup

#### For ICT & Deep Tech Startups
**A. R&D, Prototype Creation and Product Development Support**
- One-time support of 25% up to INR 25 lakhs
- Reimbursed upon submission of first sales invoice

**B. Patent Assistance**
- 75% of patent costs, up to INR 5 lakhs (domestic) or INR 10 lakhs (international)
- Maximum 10 patents per year for 5 years

**C. Quality Certification Support**
- 50% subvention for maximum 3 certifications, up to INR 5 lakhs per certificate

**D. Shared Infrastructure Support**
- 35% of bandwidth and cloud costs for 6 months
- Up to INR 10 lakhs (non-incubated) or INR 7.5 lakhs (incubated)

**E. Lease Rental Support**
- Up to INR 25/sq. ft. or actual rental (whichever lower) for 5 years
- Non-empaneled spaces: up to 15% of monthly rental or INR 1,250 per seat

**F. Special Incentives**
- As defined under section 2.7.2

**Note:** After commercialization, eligible to claim incentives as "Eligible Unit"

### 2.7.13 Incentives for Global In-House Center (GIC)/Global Capability Center (GCC)
**CAPEX and OPEX Support:** As per section 2.7.1 (1 or 2)

### Quick Policy Snapshot (For Visitors)

- **Primary Focus:** Attract and scale **deep‑tech IT/ITeS units** in Gujarat with special emphasis on AI, IoT, analytics, AR/VR, blockchain, and quantum.  
- **Who Benefits:**  
  - Eligible IT/ITeS units (Category I & II)  
  - Data centers and cloud infrastructure projects  
  - ICT & deep‑tech incubators and accelerators  
  - ICT & deep‑tech startups (including early‑stage)  
  - Global In‑House Centers (GIC/GCC)  
- **Typical Support Levels:**  
  - CAPEX: **20–30%** on eligible investments (with category‑wise caps)  
  - OPEX: **15%** for 5 years with bandwidth/cloud caps  
  - Deep‑tech startups: **25% R&D grant up to INR 25L**, plus patent, certification, and rental support.  

## Section 2.7.4: Centers of Excellence

### 2.7.4 A. Deep Tech Centre of Excellence
Advanced infrastructure with GPUs compute capacity, established in collaboration with Deep Tech industry and leading academic institutions. Focus on R&D for AI-based solutions, skill development, IP creation/management/protection/valuation/commercialization.

**CAPEX & OPEX Support:** As per section 2.7.1 (1 or 2)

### 2.7.4 B. Upskilling Industry-Ready Talent
Incentivization for undergraduate/graduate students and working professionals to complete globally recognized ICT courses. Up to INR 50,000 per course or 50% of course fee, whichever lower.

## Section 2.7.5: Infrastructure Development

### A. IT City/Township
Non-fiscal incentives:
- Relaxation in zoning and land use laws (mixed land use)
- Assistance in obtaining statutory clearances
- Relaxation in Floor Space Index (FSI) for IT office space
- Utilities/infrastructure support up to doorstep

### B. IT/ITeS Park
**CAPEX Support:**
- Up to 25% of eligible CAPEX, maximum INR 100 Cr.
- Reimbursement towards actual expenditure for IT office space creation
- Disbursement in 20 equal quarterly instalments

## Section 2.7.7: Cloud Ecosystem Support

### Cable Landing Station (CLS)
**CAPEX Support:** Up to 25% of eligible CAPEX, maximum INR 20 Cr., disbursed in 20 quarterly instalments
- Expenditure on civil works for building construction and network hardware (excluding cable laying)

**OPEX Support:** Power tariff subsidy of INR 1/unit for 5 years

## Section 2.7.8: Data Centers
**CAPEX & OPEX Support:** As per section 2.7.1 (1 or 2)

**Special Incentives:** As defined under section 2.7.2

## Section 2.7.9: R&D Support

### Support to R&D Institutes/R&D Centers
**CAPEX Support:** Up to 60% of cost for machinery, equipment, hardware, software for R&D activities, maximum INR 5 Cr.
- Provided R&D institute recognized by CSIR/DSIR

## Section 2.7.10: Non-Fiscal Incentives

- **Facilitation of Land Allotment:** Government facilitates allotment subject to availability and eligibility
- **Self-Certification:** Allowed without inspections under Factory Act, Maternity Act, Minimum Wages Act (barring complaints/random checks)
- **Investor Facilitation Center:** 24x7 helpdesk, common platform linking industries with stakeholders
- **Preferential Market Access:** Opportunities for Gujarat-based IT/ITeS units in public tenders

## Section 3.2: Implementation

### 3.2.3 Technical Committee
SLEC shall form the technical committee for validation and scrutiny of eligible entities and use cases, recommending to SLEC. Post scrutiny and validation, committee recommends eligibility of entities and use cases.

## Annexure A: Deep Tech Hardware & Software Requirements

### Indicative List (Not Exhaustive)

#### 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI), including NLP, ML, Generative AI
**Hardware:** High-Performance GPUs, High-Performance CPUs, High-capacity RAM, SSD/NVMe Storage, High-speed Network & Bandwidth

**Software:** Frameworks, Programming Languages, SDKs & Libraries, Operating Systems, Development Environments, Big Data Platforms, Data Warehousing/Visualization Tools, Data Processing Frameworks, Collaboration Tools

**Hardware:** AR Headsets, Smartphones/Tablets, Cameras and Sensors, High-speed Network Connectivity

**Software:** AR Development Kits (ARKit, ARCore), 3D Modeling Software, Programming Languages (C#, C++, Swift)

#### 4. Virtual Reality (VR) & Mixed Reality (MR)
**Hardware:** VR/MR Headsets

**Software:** VR Development Tools, 3D Modeling Software, Programming Languages (C#, C++)

#### 5. Distributed Ledger Technology (Blockchain)
**Hardware:** High-performance Nodes/Servers, Cryptographic Hardware Modules, High-speed Network Equipment

**Software:** Blockchain Platforms, Smart Contract Languages, Wallets, Security Frameworks, Database Solutions

#### 6. Quantum Computing
**Hardware:** Quantum-based Processors, Cryogenic Systems, High-performance Classical Computers, High-speed Network for Interfacing

**Software:** Quantum Development Kits, Programming Languages (Python, Q#, Cirq), Simulation Tools, Compiling Frameworks

## Policy Amendments Summary

### Key Changes (Effective 11/10/2024)

1. **Definition Changes:** Complete restructuring of ITeS to focus on Deep Technologies
2. **Employee Threshold:** Increased from 10/15 to 50/75 with 80% IT device allocation
3. **Patent Support:** Increased from INR 5 lakhs to INR 10 lakhs
4. **GFCI Definition:** Added "purchase of building" inclusion
5. **Data Center Criteria:** Added power consumption alternative (900 KW or 2,00,000 cores)
6. **CAPEX Split:** Separated building (20%) and IT equipment (30%) incentives
7. **OPEX Caps:** Added Bandwidth and Cloud Rental caps
8. **Deep Tech Focus:** One-third relaxation and extended depreciation
9. **New Entity Types:** Incubators, Accelerators, Startups, GIC/GCC definitions
10. **Technical Committee:** Added for deep tech validation

## Implementation and Compliance

### State Level Empowered Committee (SLEC)
Final decision-making authority for eligibility determination under the policy.

### Application Process
- Registration through designated portals
- Technical committee validation for deep tech entities
- Quarterly disbursement of incentives
- Regular compliance monitoring and audits

### Monitoring and Evaluation
- Performance tracking against policy objectives
- Impact assessment and mid-term corrections
- Annual reporting and policy review
